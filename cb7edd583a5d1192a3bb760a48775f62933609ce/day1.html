How learning about the worldâ€™s structure can optimise perception

Our sensory world contains statistical regularities and we use those regularities to shape what we perceive. In this talk, I will present psychophysical, modelling and neuroimaging (EEG and 7T MRI) work from the lab that considers how. The proposed mechanisms involve using predictions about what is likely to be there to render perception (1) accurate in the face of continuous streams of noisy information, and (2) optimised for future learning. How humans achieve optimisation on both fronts is a tricky puzzle to solve, and I will present the possibilities we are examining.

Deciphering Visual Processing: Integrative Approaches in Biological and Computational Vision Systems

Approximately 40% of the human cerebral cortex is dedicated to processing visual information, emphasizing the complex and crucial role of visual perception. This lecture series will explore how the visual system interprets complex visual inputs and integrates these insights into overall visual perception. Alongside our biological exploration, we will examine the foundational principles of deep learning for visual tasks, focusing specifically on the architecture and functionalities of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Inspired by biological visual processes, CNNs not only transform the field of artificial vision but also provide a quantitative framework for studying the coding schemes and functional organization of the visual system. Our discussions will include the latest advancements in this area, enriched with insights from my own research, to provide a thorough overview of our current understanding and the potential future developments in visual neuroscience.
